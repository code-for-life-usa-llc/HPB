Why is Police Brutality a problem?

Police brutality refers to the excessive use of force by a police officer against a victim or victims that is
deemed to go beyond the level required to sustain life, avoid injury, or control a situation.1encounters with
the police do not involve violence. Implicit bias training takes the approach that police officers operate with subconscious
biases that they may not even be aware of. Some police offers might think differently depending on the look of the
situation, for example, it can be really dark outside and very hard to see or it can be really bright outside and visible.

Mental Health Issues

Mental health issues play a big role in the way police act on the job. Police officers wth more work experience
are more likely to have post trauamtic stress disorder. And officers with PTSD are more likely to have a startled response
to certain situations which could lead to a gun being pulled out or unnecessary force due to being startled. Police are
better on the job when they can control their emotions under stressful situations. This begins by providing adequate funding to support the mental
health of police officers, and it also means reducing stigma and encouraging police officers to come forward when they are struggling
with their mental health.
