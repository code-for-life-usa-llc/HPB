What is police Brutality?

Us Team HPB have research to inform you U guys about police brutality. Police brutality refers to excessive use of force by the police that results in the injury or death of civilians. It can be physical or verbal and can happen during arrests, interrogations or other police interactions. Police brutality is a serious concern because it undermines the rule of law and violates the rights of citizens.

How can you help with police brutality?

People can help address police brutality by educating themselves about the issue, advocating for police reform and accountability, donating to organizations that support victims and promote police changes, participating in discussions with community leaders and law enforcement, and attending peaceful protests.

Some victims of police brutality

There have been many victims of police brutality over the years, including but not limited to: Eric Garner, Rayshard Brooks, Breonna Taylor, Tamir Rice, and George Floyd.
